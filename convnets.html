Convolutional Neural Networks for pneumonia detection

Tools used: Python (tensorflow, keras, numpy, pandas)

Objective

To investigate the notion of convolutional neural networks and its application in pneumonia detection.

Results

Convolutional Neural Networks are a powerful tool in medical imaging for the detection and classification of various diseases, including pneumonia. For our training and testing purposes, we used a dataset of labeled X-ray images sourced from Kaggle. We studied two prominent CNN architectures, namely AlexNet and VGG-16. After implementing these architectures using TensorFlow and conducting training on the dataset, we obtained the following results on the test set:

AlexNet VGG-16
Accuracy 73.1% 83.3%
Precision 70.3% 79.2%
Recall 98.5% 99.5%
F1 score 82% 88.2%
