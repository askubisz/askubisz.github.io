Pong AI

Tools used: Python (pygame, neat-python)

Objective

The project's objective is to develop an AI model capable of challenging human players. To accomplish this, we selected the game of Pong, which we implemented from scratch using PyGame in Python. Our general goal is to explore various approaches to building reinforcement learning models, including search algorithms, Q-learning, and the NEAT algorithm.

Results

In our Pong game we implemented 3 different modes:

  • PVP
  • Train AI
  • VS AI
PVP offers standard gameplay between two players, where one moves with W and S keys to go up and down, while the other uses arrow keys. In train AI mode, user can build its own reinforcement learning model. The customisable parameters to be chosen by the user are: number of generations for which the neural network will be trained, the activation function used and the type of opponent to be trained against. It can either be trained against itself, or a paddle that strictly follows the y-coordinate of the ball (more challenging). Once the training is finished, the model will get saved to a separate directory

In the VS AI mode, we have a chance to test our models (we pretrained 8 different models for convenience). The user can choose here one of 3 different types of games: AI vs hardcoded, AI vs AI and Player vs AI. We conducted a tournament among the eight pretrained models, and surprisingly, the models trained for fewer generations generally performed better. In the end, we found it impossible for a human player to beat the best AI model.
