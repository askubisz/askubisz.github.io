Face to Sketch/Sketch to face

Tools used: Python (tensorflow, keras, numpy, matplotlib)

Objective

The main goal of this project was to explore and compare various deep learning models for face sketch synthesis. In addition to applying existing architectures, we aimed to develop a novel approach by employing transfer learning with autoencoders.

Results

We used Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK) student face database as our base for training process. We split it in around 70/30 proportion into train and test sets. Our approach involved experimenting with three established generative neural network architectures: sparse autoencoders, convolutional autoencoders, and Pix2Pix (a type of Generative Adversarial Network). Additionally, we explored transfer learning by fine-tuning the VGG-16 model using our database. The results for the face-to-sketch task are presented below.

Pix2Pix emerged as the top performer among the models we examined, which aligns with expectations considering its intricate architecture. Conversely, the sparse autoencoder struggled to consistently generate accurate face sketches. Furthermore, we trained these same models for the sketch-to-face task, reversing the direction of synthesis.
